I'm Kyle Tress, an Olympic skeleton athlete and software developer @instaviser. When I'm not racing headfirst down a mountain of ice at 90mph, I like to read, write, and code.

What I'm Doing Now

After 13 seasons of skeleton, I’m taking a much-needed break from competition. This season I’ll be coaching, testing new equipment, and preparing for next year.

At the moment, the rest of my time is devoted to: - Training for the 2018 Winter Olympic Games. - Working for Instaviser.com. - Personal programming projects like sledsheet.com. - Chairing the Athlete Advisory Council of the USABS. - Reading and recommending books.

I’m often asked to do other things, especially app development and web dev/design. I appreciate the thought, but I have to decline so I can focus on my work. As my priorities change, I’ll update this page.

The inspiration for this page came from Derek Sivers. It’s a reminder for me to focus on a few essential things.
